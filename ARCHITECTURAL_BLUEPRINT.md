As stated in our vision, we aim for a system where "every summary makes the next one better." This document outlines the architecture to achieve that vision, moving beyond simple NLP to create a self-improving, globally coherent knowledge model.

## 2. Core Architecture: The Global Knowledge Graph (GKG)

The heart of the 'SUM' engine is the **Global Knowledge Graph (GKG)**. The GKG is a persistent, evolving, and interconnected model of all knowledge the system has ever processed.

*   **Structure:** The GKG is a directed graph where nodes represent concepts, entities, and information atoms. Edges represent the relationships, context, and semantic meaning between them.
*   **Ingestion:** When new information is processed (a document, an article, a user's note), it is not merely summarized. It is decomposed, understood, and integrated into the GKG. New concepts are added, and existing ones are enriched and re-contextualized.
*   **Learning:** The GKG is not static. It is a learning entity. With every new piece of information, the GKG refines its understanding of the world. The "Global Knowledge Graph" will be the mechanism that enables the system to get better with time.

## 3. The Distillation & Extrapolation Engine

Summarization is no longer a simple extraction of sentences. It is an act of querying the GKG and generating a response tailored to the user's request.

*   **The 'Granularity Slider' Redefined:** The "Granularity Slider" is no longer a control for sentence count. It is a dial that controls the level of abstraction and extrapolation.
    *   **Level 1 (Atom):** Extracts key entities and concepts (the most fundamental nodes in the GKG).
    *   **Level 5 (Distillation):** Generates a concise summary, a narrative woven from the most relevant concepts in the GKG.
    *   **Level 10 (Extrapolation):** Goes beyond the source text. It surfaces related concepts, identifies hidden patterns, poses questions, and generates novel insights based on the vast interconnected knowledge within the GKG. This is how we move from information to intelligence.

## 4. The Learning Loop: The Engine of Improvement

The system's ability to "get better with time" is driven by a continuous learning loop.

1.  **Process:** Ingest and integrate information into the GKG.
2.  **Generate:** Create summaries and extrapolations based on the current state of the GKG.
3.  **Feedback:** Every user interaction provides feedback. Did the user expand a section? Did they ask a follow-up question? This data is used to refine the GKG.
4.  **Refine:** The GKG is constantly optimized based on feedback and new information, strengthening connections, and pruning redundancies.
