Glossary of Key Terms:
* Ahl as-Sunna: Sunni Muslims, considered by the author to be the true followers of Islam.
* As'hab: The companions of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).
* Ayat: Verses from the Quran.
* Bid'at: Innovation in religious matters, often viewed negatively as a deviation from the Prophet's teachings.
* Dalalat: Aberration, going astray from the right path.
* Din: Religion, way of life.
* Fard: Obligatory act in Islam.
* Hadith: Sayings and traditions of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).
* Halal: Permitted or lawful in Islam.
* Haram: Forbidden or unlawful in Islam.
* Hidayat: Guidance to the right path.
* Ijtihad: Independent reasoning in Islamic jurisprudence.
* Iman: Faith, belief in the articles of Islamic faith.
* Islam: Submission to the will of God.
* Kafir: Disbeliever, one who rejects Islam.
* Kalimat ash-shahada: The Islamic declaration of faith: "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is His Messenger."
* Madhhab: School of thought within Islamic jurisprudence (e.g., Hanafi, Maliki, Shafii, Hanbali).
* Mahram: A close relative whom one is forbidden to marry.
* Makruh: Disliked or reprehensible act in Islam.
* Mumin: Believer, one who has iman.
* Murtadd: Apostate, one who leaves Islam.
* Mut'a nikah: Temporary marriage, considered permissible by some groups but forbidden by Sunnis.
* Nafs: The lower self, desires and ego.
* Rukhsat: Permission, the easier way in carrying out a religious obligation.
* Salat: The five daily prayers in Islam.
* Sayyid: Descendant of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) through his grandsons, Hasan and Husayn.
* Shariat: Islamic law.
* Shii: Shiite Muslims, followers of a branch of Islam that considers Ali ibn Abi Talib, the Prophet's cousin and son-in-law, to be his rightful successor.
* Sirat al-mustaqim: The straight path, referring to the path of righteousness in Islam.
* Sunna: The Prophet Muhammad's teachings, practices, and sayings.
* Taj Mahal: A mausoleum in India, used in the text as an example of British looting during their colonial rule.
* Tawba: Repentance, seeking forgiveness from God.
* Wahhabi: Followers of a strict interpretation of Islam that emerged in 18th-century Arabia.
* Zindiq: Heretic, one who conceals disbelief while pretending to be Muslim.
